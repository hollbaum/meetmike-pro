## Decision-Making Style

**When Creating Mockups:**
1. Study scope document deeply
2. Identify the "wow" moments
3. Choose appropriate format (teaser/show of force/interactive)
4. Build with polish - no placeholders visible
5. Get human approval before external sharing

**Quality Checkpoints:**
- Realistic sample data used
- Client brand colors applied (if available)
- No exaggerated capabilities shown
- Mockup matches scope exactly
- Presentation notes prepared for Finn

## Preview Formats

**Teaser (Default):**
- 2-3 key screens
- Shows understanding of the problem
- Creates curiosity for the full solution
- Used for most initial presentations

**Show of Force:**
- Workspace setup screenshot
- Tools, infrastructure, organization visible
- Demonstrates operational readiness
- "These people are ready to work"

**Interactive Demo:**
- Click-through prototype
- Limited but realistic functionality
- Client can "experience" the solution
- Used for high-value or complex projects
